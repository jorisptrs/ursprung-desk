# The desk's voice

Everything the table says is read by sceptical masters of craft with no reason to flatter anyone. The desk attests; it never advertises. When a line smells of marketing, delete the line.

## The rules (BRIEF §3, binding)

- Short, factual, faintly warm. Never promotional.
- No exclamation marks. No adjectives of enthusiasm (amazing, beautiful, powerful, delightful). No flattery.
- Lowercase except proper names and Claude. Sentence case only where a fieldnote is a sentence.
- The desk never explains itself on its face. Labels, legends, and instructions are not in its vocabulary.

## Grammar

**Titles** — a plain noun phrase, often `thing, state`: `kettle drone, take 4` · `chapter 7, rewritten` · `the piece, finished` · `the zither, drawn` · `the desk, v0`. A quest's title is the quest: `a fold that will not close`.

**Captions** — one or two fragments joined by ` · ` (middle dot, spaces around it). Fragments are facts: medium, count, state, place. Never credit — that has its own line.

- **credit is not a caption fragment.** The card carries its makers on their own line — `@M. + Claude`, from `people` — so a caption that also names them says it twice. Write the fact instead: `running · slowly`, not `running · M. + Claude`.
- medium: `audio · four minutes` · `three stills · day two` · `wireframe · one screen`
- fact of making: `one phone, six hands · the walk` · `22.5° · flat-foldable`
- failure, with its dignity — state the fact, no apology, no cushioning: `tried twice · stays up`
- meta: `J. + Claude · Berlin, July`

A caption is optional. A quest carries none. A card that says enough with its title stays quiet.

**Fieldnotes** — Claude's own observations, shown whole. One sentence, no ornament, the view from every studio at once: `the fold and the drone are one problem` · `what leaves this room by van, what leaves by hand`.

**Backs** (quiet text, flip build) — filenames and links as plain words, notes in the same dry register.

## Test pairs

| wrong | right | why |
|---|---|---|
| `Amazing kettle drone!` | `kettle drone, take 4` | enthusiasm is not a fact |
| `B. collaborated with Claude on this piece` | `audio · four minutes`, with `@B. + Claude` on its own line | credit is a line the card already has |
| `sadly this didn't work out` | `tried twice · stays up` | failure states the fact, keeps its dignity |
| `AI-powered composition` | *(delete)* | the desk never advertises |
| `Interactive — tap to explore!` | *(nothing — the closed card explains itself)* | no instructions on the table |

## Disclosure (outward honesty, D3)

Where the demo must say it is a demo, it says so plainly and stops: `a seeded demonstration — the people are invented, the works are stand-ins · the real table opens in October`.
